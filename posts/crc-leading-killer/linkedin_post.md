The Guardian reported that colorectal cancer is now the leading cause of cancer death in Americans under 50. We checked 44 years of Nordic registry data to see how this looks in Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden.

CRC incidence in under-50s has roughly doubled since 1980 in both sexes: +140% in males, +90% in females. The rise predates screening programmes entirely and shows no sign of plateauing. In young men, CRC's share of all cancer diagnoses has climbed from 7% to nearly 10%, meaning it is outpacing cancer incidence in general.

But the headline does not translate directly. CRC ranks #3 among cancer deaths in under-50s for both sexes in the Nordics, behind brain tumours and leukaemia in men, and behind breast cancer in women. The US-specific ranking likely reflects differences in screening, dietary patterns, and the relative burden of other cancers.

What the Nordic data does confirm is the direction. The trajectory is unmistakable, and whether CRC reaches #1 matters less than the fact that it is accelerating in a population where screening offers no protection.
