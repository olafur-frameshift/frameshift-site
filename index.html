Who we are

Statistics first.

We are methodologists. Study design, analysis plans, and interpretation follow from the statistics, not the other way around.

Ólafur B. Davíðsson

Co-Founder & Lead Biostatistician

Statistical epidemiologist with a PhD from the University of Copenhagen. He trained in statistical genetics at deCODE genetics and held research posts at Statens Serum Institut and the Danish Cancer Institute before co-founding Frameshift. He has led the statistical work on randomized clinical trials, nationwide Danish registry studies, and genetic epidemiology, including research published in NEJM Evidence, the British Journal of Cancer, and The Lancet Obstetrics, Gynaecology & Women's Health. At Frameshift he leads study design, analysis strategy, statistical programming, and reporting.

How we help

Shaping the question and analysis

We work with teams that have data and broad objectives, and want to shape clear analytical paths. That often means clarifying what can credibly be supported and which analyses are worth pursuing.

Statistical planning and trial analysis

Clear, decision-ready SAPs, trial analyses, and reporting. Estimands, endpoints, analysis populations, and strategy are defined at the planning stage—not renegotiated mid-analysis.

Registry and real-world evidence

Design and execution of observational studies using health registers and other real-world data. Credible results rely on attention to bias, timing, and comparability.
