NEJM Evidence 2024

Intact Fish Skin Graft to Treat Deep Diabetic Foot Ulcers

A randomised controlled trial across 255 patients at multiple international centres testing intact fish skin grafts against standard wound care for diabetic foot ulcers penetrating to bone, joint, or tendon. Fish skin grafts achieved wound closure in 44% of patients at 16 weeks versus 26% with standard care, establishing a meaningful new treatment option for one of the most challenging wound types in diabetic medicine.

British Journal of Cancer 2024

Infections and Their Prognostic Significance Before Diagnosis of CLL, Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma, or Multiple Myeloma

A Danish registry study of nearly 30,000 patients showing that infection burden is elevated up to 15 years before diagnosis of B-cell malignancies. More than two antimicrobial prescriptions in the year before diagnosis were independently associated with shorter overall survival across all three malignancies — suggesting that immune dysfunction manifests years before the cancer becomes clinically apparent.

The Lancet Regional Health — Europe 2025

Epidemiological and Genetic Evidence for Shared Mechanisms Between Migraine and Pre-eclampsia

A nationwide Danish cohort and genetic risk study combining registry data from the full Danish population with genetic risk score analyses to investigate whether migraine and pre-eclampsia share biological mechanisms. The study finds epidemiological and genetic evidence for overlapping aetiological pathways, with implications for cardiovascular risk stratification in women with a history of either condition.
