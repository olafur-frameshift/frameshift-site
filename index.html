Intact Fish Skin Graft to Treat Deep Diabetic Foot Ulcers

Randomised trial across 255 patients testing fish skin grafts against standard care for deep diabetic foot ulcers. Fish skin achieved wound closure in 44% versus 26% at 16 weeks.

Danish registry study of 30,000 patients showing infection burden is elevated up to 15 years before diagnosis of B-cell malignancies, with implications for early immune dysfunction and prognosis.
